The question

Humanitarian coordinators and donor advisors need rapid, defensible answers to one recurring question:

Relative to documented need, which crises are most overlooked?

Typical decision-maker queries:

  • Which crises have the highest people-in-need but the lowest fund allocations?
  • Which countries with active HRPs have negligible funding?
  • Which regions are consistently underfunded across multiple years?
  • Which food-security hotspots received under 10% of requirements?

Part I

Approach

Core insight

Existing tools (CERF UFE, FTS coverage ratios, Palantir Foundry/DOTS) score overlookedness as a scalar — one number per country-year.

Our contribution: a joint distribution view, with two new axes on top of the standard gap score:
  • Intra-crisis equity — how unequally is coverage distributed across sector clusters within one country?
  • Inter-stakeholder disagreement — how much does a crisis's rank shift across plausible donor-preference profiles?

Three papers independently flag equity-aware modelling as an explicit open gap (Abdulrashid 2026 §7.3; Sahebjamnia 2017 §6; Vargas Florez 2015 Eqs. 9–12, which provides the equation template).

The gap score

Extending the MADM/AHP/MAUT framework (Rye & Aktas, 2022). For each eligible crisis u, under profile p:

\[G_p(u) \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{6} w_{p,i} \cdot U_i\!\left(\tilde{a}_i(u)\right)\]

Six attributes

  • Coverage shortfall
  • Per-PIN funding gap
  • Need intensity (PIN / population)
  • Severity (HNO / IPC)
  • Donor concentration (HHI)
  • Intra-crisis equity (Gini across clusters) — new

Four donor profiles

  • CERF-flavoured — severity + magnitude
  • ECHO-flavoured — equity-weighted
  • USAID-flavoured — donor-concentration lens
  • NGO-consortium — cluster-equity dominant

Score computed under every profile; we report median rank + inter-quartile range.

Third axis: structural vs. acute

The typology is a point-in-time view. For the bonus task we add a temporal axis:

\[\bar{G}_k(u) = \tfrac{1}{k}\!\!\sum_{t=T-k+1}^{T}\! G_t(u) \quad\quad \Delta G(u) = G_T(u) - \tfrac{1}{k-1}\!\!\sum_{t=T-k+1}^{T-1}\! G_t(u)\]

Chronic (\(\bar{G}_k\))

Persistent underfunding across k years. Policy response: multi-year pooled funding.

Acute (\(\Delta G\))

Recent deviation from rolling mean. Policy response: CERF rapid-response window.

Connects the policy recommendation directly to the temporal shape of the gap, addressing the bonus task explicitly.

Limitations & ethics

  • Decision support, not automation. The tool ranks; humans decide.
  • Profile weights are illustrative. The four profiles are stylised mandate emphases, not endorsed by any institution.
  • Visibility bias. Crises with collapsed statistical systems may rank lower because HNO data is stale or missing — flagged, not corrected.
  • Political sensitivity. Labels like "contested" carry implications. Outputs report numeric facts; never donor-shaming framing.
  • No false precision. Two significant figures; IQR always shown alongside point estimates.
