Which humanitarian crises are most overlooked?
Date: 19 April 2026

The question

Relative to documented need, which crises are most overlooked?

Humanitarian coordinators and donor advisors need a defensible answer every cycle. No existing tool gives them one.

Geo-Insight2

The analytical problem

When every response is underfunded, "underfunded" stops being useful.

A single coverage ratio can't discriminate between 19 contexts that are all below 50 %. The interesting variation is no longer whether a crisis is underfunded — but how, and for whom.

Geo-Insight5

How the score is computed

Overlookedness is a latent scalar $\theta(u)$ — never observed directly. Each of six signals is a noisy view of $\theta$; the model writes down what each signal looks like given $\theta$, and inverts.

\[a_i \mid \theta \;\sim\; \begin{cases} \text{Beta}\bigl(\sigma(\alpha_i + \beta_i\theta)\,\phi_i,\; (1-\sigma(\alpha_i + \beta_i\theta))\,\phi_i\bigr) & i \in \{1,3,5,6\} \\[0.2em] \text{LogNormal}(\alpha_2 + \beta_2\theta,\; \sigma_2^2) & i = 2 \\[0.2em] \text{OrderedLogistic}(\alpha_4 + \beta_4\theta,\; \kappa) & i = 4 \end{cases}\]
θ(u)latent overlookedness — what we want to know
aiobserved attribute i, on its natural support
βi > 0slope: how strongly attribute i moves with θ
σ, φ, κlink function, dispersion, ordinal cut-points

Three likelihoods, six attributes: Beta regression for the four bounded fractions (coverage shortfall, need intensity, donor concentration, sector inequality); log-normal for the per-PIN gap; ordered logistic for INFORM severity.
Honest under incompleteness. Missing observations contribute no likelihood term. The posterior on θ for a sparse-data country is wider — not shifted, not zero-imputed — because there is genuinely less evidence.

Geo-Insight6

From raw data to score

71 typed properties, arranged in five derivation levels. Every score traces back to its source.

L5 9 Posterior Bayesian posterior over latent θ · 90% credible interval · crisis typology
L4 12 Trends Rolling baselines · year-on-year change · acceleration
L3 10 Inequality Sector and donor distribution indices · weakest-sector coverage
L2 18 Ratios Scale-normalised signals · coverage · gap per person in need
L1 22 Observations Raw counts and US-dollar figures as published (HNO · HRP · FTS · INFORM)
derived observed

Each derived property declares its formula, its source, and its known failure modes — before a score is ever computed.

Geo-Insight8

When data is missing

Most crises lack at least one of the six signals. Three possible approaches, one is defensible.

DROP INCOMPLETE

Wasteful

Only 18 of 114 eligible countries receive a score. The other 96 disappear silently.

RENORMALISE

Unstable

Rescaling to the observed signals lets partly-documented countries with one extreme value displace the true top. Against the CERF benchmark, top-5 overlap drops from 100 % to 0 %.

EVIDENCE-BOUNDED — our choice

Robust

Missing signals contribute zero. A score is therefore bounded by the evidence that supports it. Against the same benchmark, we preserve 80 % top-5 overlap while scoring 57 countries instead of 18.

Direct consequence of the formula: we sum, we do not renormalise. The accepted cost is systematic under-ranking of poorly-documented crises — never inflation.

Geo-Insight9
Part II

Does it agree with reality?

Two independent benchmarks

Human-curated lists from two organisations that do not use our method:

OCHA

CERF Underfunded Emergencies

Twice-yearly allocation to protracted or neglected humanitarian crises where funding is low but vulnerability is high. 17 countries selected in 2025, 22 across 2024–25.

Validates the underfunding axis.

CARE

Breaking the Silence 2024

Annual top ten of the most under-reported humanitarian crises. Measured by media attention — not by funding.

Validates the forgotten-crisis signal, partially and by design.

Evaluation: precision in the top K, recall of each benchmark, rank correlation on the intersection, and stability as the pool size grows.
