The question

Humanitarian coordinators and donor advisors need rapid, defensible answers to one recurring question:

Relative to documented need, which crises are most overlooked?

Typical decision-maker queries:

  • Which crises have the highest people-in-need but the lowest fund allocations?
  • Which countries with active HRPs have negligible funding?
  • Which regions are consistently underfunded across multiple years?
  • Which food-security hotspots received under 10% of requirements?

The gap score

Following a multi-attribute utility framing (Rye & Aktas, 2022). For each eligible crisis u:

\[G(u) \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{5} w_i \cdot U_i\!\left(\tilde{a}_i(u)\right)\]

Attributes

  • Coverage shortfall
  • Per-PIN funding gap
  • Need intensity (PIN / population)
  • Severity (HNO / IPC)
  • Donor concentration (HHI)

Properties

  • Weights elicited via AHP
  • Every score decomposes into attribute contributions
  • Three default profiles: balanced, equity, donor-advisory
  • Concave utility on coverage for low-coverage tail
GEO-Insight · Datathon 20264

Structural vs. acute neglect

For the bonus task, we decompose the gap score temporally:

\[\bar{G}_k(u) = \tfrac{1}{k}\!\!\sum_{t=T-k+1}^{T}\! G_t(u) \quad\quad \Delta G(u) = G_T(u) - \tfrac{1}{k-1}\!\!\sum_{t=T-k+1}^{T-1}\! G_t(u)\]

Chronic axis

Persistent underfunding across k years. Policy response: multi-year pooled funding.

Acute axis

Recent deviation from rolling mean. Policy response: CERF rapid-response window.

Limitations & ethics

  • Decision support, not automation. The tool ranks; humans decide.
  • Visibility bias. Crises with collapsed statistical systems may appear under-ranked because HNO data is stale or missing.
  • Political sensitivity. Outputs report numeric facts and attribute contributions, never donor-shaming framing.
  • No false precision. Scores to two significant figures; sensitivity bands shown alongside point values.
  • Reproducibility. Data fetches timestamped and versioned; LLM calls logged.
