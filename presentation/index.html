The analytical problem

When every response is underfunded,
"underfunded" stops being useful.

A single coverage ratio can't discriminate between 19 contexts that are all below 50 %. The interesting variation is no longer whether a crisis is underfunded — but how, and for whom.

How the score works

For each crisis, six signals are weighted by a donor profile and aggregated over observed attributes:

\[G_p(u) \;=\; \sum_{i \in \mathcal{O}(u)} w_{p,i} \cdot U_i\!\left(\tilde{a}_i(u)\right)\]
Gp(u)gap score for crisis u under profile p
𝒪(u)indices of attributes observed for u
wp,iprofile p's weight on signal i
Ui(·)utility transform for signal i
ãi(u)min-max normalised signal value

Six signals: coverage shortfall · per-PIN funding gap · need intensity · severity · donor concentration · intra-crisis equity Gini. Four profiles: CERF-, ECHO-, USAID-, NGO-flavoured.
Evidence-bounded: missing attributes contribute zero (no renormalisation). A partial-complete country's max score is bounded by its observed weight-mass — incompleteness cannot inflate rank.
Sources: 5 OCHA datasets (HNO, HRP, FTS, CoD-PS, CBPF); severity from INFORM (EU JRC, monthly, 109 countries).

GEO-Insight · Cache Me if You Can7

A third axis: time

A crisis overlooked for five years needs different policy than one that just tipped. We separate the two.

Chronic

Persistent underfunding across years. Response: multi-year pooled funding.

Acute

Recent collapse from baseline. Response: CERF rapid-response window.

Chronic = $k$-year rolling mean of the gap. Acute = deviation of this year from that rolling mean.

GEO-Insight · Cache Me if You Can8

Robustness to incomplete data

Most crises lack at least one of the six attributes. Three candidate handlings, one is defensible.

HARD-DROP

Wasteful

Only 18 / 114 eligible countries scored. The other 96 drop out silently.

REWEIGHT OBSERVED

Inflates ranks

Dividing out missing-weight mass lets partial-complete countries with extreme observed attributes displace the true top. On our CERF UFE validation: top-5 overlap collapses 100% → 0%.

EVIDENCE-BOUNDED · OUR CHOICE

Robust

Missing ≡ 0 contribution. Max score bounded by observed weight-mass. Pool 57 preserves 80% CERF overlap — within one country of the strict pool.

This choice follows directly from equation (1) on the previous slide: we sum, we do not renormalise. The cost — systematic under-ranking of poorly-documented crises — is the accepted flip side of refusing to extrapolate beyond observation.

Does it agree with reality?

We don't validate against ourselves

Two lists, human-curated by independent organisations, not using our method:

OCHA

CERF Underfunded Emergencies

Twice-yearly allocation to "protracted or neglected humanitarian crises where funding is low but vulnerability is high." 17 countries selected in 2025 across both windows; 22 across 2024–25 combined.

→ validates our underfunding axis.

CARE

Breaking the Silence 2024

Annual ranked top-10 of the most under-reported humanitarian crises. Measured by media attention — not by funding.

→ validates our sector-starved / forgotten axis (partially, by design — different signal).

Metrics: precision at top-K · recall of benchmark · Spearman ρ on the intersection · stability across pool sizes.

GEO-Insight · Cache Me if You Can12

What this is not

NOT AUTOMATION

The tool ranks. Humans decide. A score is a hypothesis, never a verdict.

NOT A SINGLE NUMBER

Four donor profiles, four ranks per crisis. We report the median and the IQR.

NOT IMPUTED

Missing attributes contribute zero to the score, never country-specific guesses.

NOT NEUTRAL

We surface donor disagreement as signal instead of hiding it in consensus.

NOT COMPLETE

Completeness flag attached to every score. Evidence-bounded scoring cannot inflate partial-complete ranks.

NOT PRECISE

Two significant figures. A score of 0.62 is not meaningfully distinct from 0.59.
