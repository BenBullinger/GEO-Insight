Geo-Insight

Which humanitarian crises are most overlooked?

When nearly every response is underfunded, a single coverage ratio no longer discriminates between crises. Geo-Insight reframes overlookedness as a joint distribution over four donor-preference profiles, with an intra-crisis equity term and a cross-stakeholder disagreement term. Validated against two independent human-curated benchmarks.

§ 2 · The method

A typed semantic layer from raw data to score.

L5 9 Scorescomposite gap scores, typology
L4 12 Trendsbaselines, change over time
L3 10 Inequalitysector + donor spread
L2 18 Ratiosscale-normalised signals
L1 22 Observationsraw HNO · HRP · FTS · INFORM

Seventy-one properties in five derivation levels. Every derived value declares its formula, its source dataset, and its known failure modes before a score is computed.

Aggregation is evidence-bounded: missing signals contribute zero rather than being rescaled, so missing data cannot inflate a country's rank. Four donor-preference profiles produce four ranks per crisis; the median and the inter-quartile range are reported side by side.

This is the substrate on which every ranking and every lens view in the analysis site is built — nothing appears on screen that isn't registered in the ontology.

Tripling the pool by admitting partly-documented crises costs one country out of the top five. The method surfaces incompleteness explicitly; partly-observed crises are never assigned a higher rank than their evidence supports.
